Experience
IBM: Back End Intern

Worked on updating and maintaining Hyperledger Fabric.

Back End Intern

IBM

Durham, NC
June 15th - August 7th 2020
The internship was 100% virtual due to COVID. I was in the IBM Blockchain department, specifically working in Hyperledger Fabric. Fabric is an open source Blockchain platform used for secure and private transactions between different users ie: supply chains. The learning curve is fairly steep, fortunately, everyone was very helpful and willing to share their knowledge with me.
I spent the majority of the internship working in Fabric-Samples. Samples is in the tutorial side of Fabric. Developers interested in learning about Fabric will start in Samples to play around with sample code. I developed sample applications and smart contracts for users to practice with and take a deeper look at. The samples are often used by companies as templates for their deployed apps and contracts.
An applications is the API for a user in a Fabric network. It is how a user can access the network and invoke their smart contract. I developed a couple sample applications in Java: app1 & app2. I became the go to Java person in my squad.

Smart Contracts are the business logic for making transactions in a Fabric network. They define the rules of the transactions and are used by users to interact with the ledger, ie: queries and commits. I developed sample smart contracts in Java and JavaScript. For the Java contract, I used Gradle as a build tool, and used Junit and Mockito for unit testing. For both contracts I used a test network for integration testing. After development, I would make a Github PR and work with maintainers to get it merged into the main Fabric-Samples repo.
Overall it was an enriching experience. I learned so much in such a short period and got to meet many driven, kind, and ambitious people. From the people in my squad, all the way up to the CTO of the department, everyone I spoke to was open and helpful. I spoke to countless IBMers that had been at IBM their whole career, or switched into IBM and never looked back. It all speaks volumes to the culture IBM has built within their company. I'm very grateful for my time there, and who knows, maybe I'll be an IBMer again down the road.

Pika Profession

Pika Profession

Developed for the IBM NA Intern 2020 Hackathon 4th/26

Group of 5
Front-end & Back-end

5 interns grouped up for 2 days of caffiene-fueled mayhem. Developed in Bootstrap for frontend and Firebase for backend. Employers can register to post jobs and view resumes. Applicants register and can view jobs they're interested in. Applicants upload their resume and Watson AI blacks out their name anywhere on the resume. Once uploaded and blacked out, the employer takes a look at the resume and simply judges it off of the candidate's merit. If the employer is interested they can initiate a chat through a built in messaging service. Our names can be very telling of our gender, race, and ethnicity, those things shouldn't matter when you're hiring for a position.

I created the Firebase project and wrote a readme for everyone on the team to get on the same page, this allowed for everyone to work in parallel and test/integrate their work. I also worked on both the front-end and backend. I styled the login and register pages and hooked it up to the Firebase authentication. I also hooked up the resume upload and storage. Users would upload their resume and it would get sent to Firebase Storage. Metadata was stored on who the resume belonged to and which jobs it had been applied to. There was a lot of learning on the fly for everyone so we weren't able to implement everything, but I'm very happy with how the result turned out.
